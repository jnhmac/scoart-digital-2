## Choosing the Right E-commerce Platform

When international brands ask us which platform to use for their US store, the honest answer is: it depends. Both Shopify Plus and WooCommerce can get you selling in America. The right choice comes down to your team, your budget, and how much control you need.

Here's a straightforward comparison based on what we've seen working with 50+ international brands.

## Shopify Plus: The Managed Approach

**Best for:** Brands that want to launch fast and focus on selling, not managing infrastructure.

### Strengths

- **US payment processing built in**: Stripe integration, tax calculation, and PCI compliance handled automatically.
- **Speed to market**: A competent team can have a store live in 2-4 weeks.
- **Reliability**: 99.99% uptime. You don't manage servers, security patches, or hosting.
- **Multi-currency support**: Native support for selling in multiple currencies from a single store.

### Trade-offs

- **Monthly cost**: Shopify Plus starts at $2,300/month. For early-stage US operations, this is a significant fixed cost.
- **Customization limits**: You can customize themes and use apps, but deep backend modifications require Shopify's proprietary Liquid templating language.
- **App dependency**: Many features require paid apps, which add up quickly.

## WooCommerce: The Flexible Approach

**Best for:** Brands that need deep customization, have technical resources, or want to keep costs variable.

### Strengths

- **Full control**: You own your code, your data, and your hosting. No platform lock-in.
- **Lower entry cost**: WordPress hosting starts at $30-100/month. WooCommerce itself is free.
- **Extensibility**: Thousands of plugins, and you can build custom functionality without platform restrictions.
- **Content marketing integration**: Built on WordPress, which means your blog, SEO content, and store share the same platform.

### Trade-offs

- **Maintenance responsibility**: You manage hosting, security updates, backups, and performance optimization.
- **Payment complexity**: You'll need to configure payment gateways, handle PCI compliance, and manage tax calculation through third-party services.
- **Scaling requires planning**: High-traffic stores need proper caching, CDN, and potentially managed WordPress hosting ($100-500/month).

## What We Recommend

For most international brands entering the US market, we recommend starting with **Shopify Plus if**:
- You have a proven product and want to reach US customers quickly
- Your team doesn't include dedicated developers
- You prefer predictable monthly costs over variable infrastructure expenses

We recommend **WooCommerce if**:
- You need deep integration with existing systems (ERP, custom CRM, proprietary tools)
- You have development resources in-house or with a partner like us
- Content marketing is central to your US market strategy
- You want maximum flexibility in how your store works

## Don't Forget OpenCart

For brands already running OpenCart internationally, it's often more practical to extend your existing platform to the US market rather than migrating. We help OpenCart merchants optimize for US payment processing, tax compliance, and shipping integration.

## The Platform Doesn't Sell. Your Strategy Does

The platform is a tool. What actually drives US sales is your product positioning, pricing strategy, marketing execution, and customer experience. We've seen brands succeed on every platform and fail on every platform.

The right question isn't "Which platform is best?" It's "Which platform lets my team execute our US market strategy most effectively?"
